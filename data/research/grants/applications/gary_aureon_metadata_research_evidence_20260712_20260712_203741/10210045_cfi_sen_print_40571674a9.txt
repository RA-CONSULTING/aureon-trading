Project and scope summary
Provide a short summary of your project.

R&A Consulting and Brokerage Services Ltd will develop Aureon SEN Evidence OS, a human-in-the-loop evidence and workflow tool to support earlier, fairer and more reliable identification of special educational needs. The project is not a diagnostic product and will not replace teacher, SENCO, healthcare or local authority judgement. It will create an auditable support layer that helps professionals and families gather, structure and review the evidence already used around strengths and needs assessment.

The current Aureon capability is a local-first evidence operating system: source inventory, claim classification, risk tagging, workflow logs, validation gates and human review states. Phase 1 will adapt that capability for SEN contexts where evidence is fragmented across observations, learning records, parent/carer input, professional notes and assessment pathways. The demonstrator will use synthetic and de-identified examples first, then run structured co-design and usability testing with relevant users and beneficiaries.

The innovation is the evidence layer, not a black-box prediction engine. It will make visible what evidence has been considered, what is missing, where confidence is weak, what safeguarding or data-protection controls apply, and what requires professional review. This can help reduce inconsistency in early identification and strengths and needs assessment without creating automated eligibility decisions.

Phase 1 will deliver a SEN evidence taxonomy, prototype workflow, professional review interface, safeguarding and data-protection pack, co-design findings, validation report, route-to-adoption plan and commercialisation pathway. R&A will work as a single legal entity and use specialist subcontracted input only where required for SEN practice, safeguarding, data protection, user research and accessibility. The project is UK based and will benefit UK children and families directly or indirectly by improving the quality, consistency and reviewability of evidence used in earlier support decisions.

6. Proposed idea or technology
How does the project meet the challenge described in the competition scope?

Aureon SEN Evidence OS is a human-in-the-loop evidence operating layer for earlier SEN identification and strengths and needs assessment. It addresses the challenge by improving how evidence is gathered, structured, reviewed and explained in contexts where existing tools underperform, do not operate, or are not trusted by all parties.

The tool will convert fragmented inputs into reviewable evidence records: classroom observations, parent/carer concerns, learner strengths, professional notes, reasonable adjustment history, speech and language indicators, assessment prompts and referral-pathway evidence. Each record will carry source, date, context, confidence, sensitivity, missing-information and review-status metadata. The interface will show what evidence supports a concern, what evidence is weak or absent, and what should be reviewed by a teacher, SENCO, relevant professional or family member.

The project is not a diagnostic engine. It will not automatically label a child, rank entitlement, or replace professional judgement. Its purpose is to make the evidence route clearer, fairer and more consistent so that professionals and families can make better-informed decisions earlier.

The current state of development is a working local Aureon evidence automation capability outside the SEN domain: source inventory, claim classification, risk tagging, workflow logs, validation gates and human review states. The SEN-specific taxonomy, safeguarding pattern, co-design workflow and assessment support interface will be developed and tested in phase 1. The project will begin with synthetic and de-identified examples, then involve appropriate child, parent/carer, teacher and SENCO engagement through consented, safeguarded co-design and usability testing.

The tangible output will be a prototype platform, SEN evidence taxonomy, safeguarding and data-protection pack, usability and validity findings, adoption playbook, commercialisation plan and phase 2 route.

7. Technical project summary
What are the main technical challenges you are addressing?

The main technical challenge is building a useful evidence workflow without creating unsafe automated decisions. R&A will address this by separating evidence organisation from decision-making: the system will structure, explain and flag evidence, while human professionals and families remain responsible for interpretation and action.

The innovation is a local-first, auditable evidence layer for SEN practice. It combines source mapping, evidence taxonomy, missing-evidence detection, sensitivity classification, review-status tracking, fairness checks, explainable prompts and professional handoff in one workflow. Current tools tend to focus on assessment content, case management or generic forms. Aureon focuses on proof, gaps, provenance and reviewability.

Technical deliverables are: SEN evidence schema, synthetic/de-identified benchmark set, ingestion and annotation prototype, review interface, safeguarding and DPIA workflow, co-design toolkit, validation report and adoption playbook. R&D will test whether the workflow improves completeness, consistency, transparency and user confidence compared with baseline manual evidence collection. Environmental impact is low and will be managed through local-first processing and limited data retention. Commercial merit will be tested through user research, public-sector adoption analysis and service-backed pilot planning.

8. Current state of the art and intellectual property
Are similar products currently available in the market?

Similar products exist in assessment, case management, education records and AI-supported administration, but the project is differentiated by focusing on auditable evidence provenance and human review rather than automated diagnosis or generic workflow storage.

The existing Aureon IP is R&A's local-first evidence automation and workflow assurance capability: source inventory, claim classification, risk tagging, validation gates, reviewer status, generated evidence packs and grant-ready metadata controls. The project will adapt that background IP into a SEN-specific evidence taxonomy, data-protection pattern, professional handoff model and usability-tested interface.

Novel concepts include structured evidence gap detection for earlier SEN support, confidence and sensitivity metadata attached to each evidence item, explicit separation of evidence support from statutory or clinical decision-making, and a review workflow that can grow with the child across age, geography and setting.

Freedom to operate will be managed through open-source licence review, dependency register, documentation of background and foreground IP, and separation of generic Aureon tooling from project-specific outputs. If specialist subcontractors are used, contracts will require assignment or appropriate licence of deliverables so R&A can fulfil the CfI contract while retaining and commercialising foreground IP as permitted.

9. Project plan and methodology
Describe your project plan and identify the main milestones.

Phase 1 runs for 12 months from 1 October 2026 to 30 September 2027. M1 sets up governance, safeguarding, DPIA planning, evidence taxonomy and success metrics. M2 designs user engagement and recruitment materials. M3 builds the synthetic/de-identified benchmark corpus. M4 develops ingestion and workflow logging. M5 builds evidence gap, sensitivity and confidence scoring. M6 delivers the human review and explanation interface. M7 runs co-design with parents/carers, teachers and SENCOs. M8 tests usability and validity across age, setting and geographic scenarios. M9 produces compliance, accessibility and integration evidence. M10 completes pilot simulation and adoption playbook. M11 completes commercial and phase 2 planning. M12 delivers the final report and funder-ready phase 2 package.

Management controls are monthly milestones, weekly build reviews, issue log, risk register, change control, spend tracking and evidence ledger. Success criteria are a working prototype, no automated diagnostic decisioning, completed safeguarding and data-protection pack, user-tested evidence taxonomy, measurable improvement in evidence completeness and review clarity, and a credible adoption route. Main risks are weak user access, sensitive data handling, over-claiming AI outputs and small-team delivery load. Mitigations are funded user research, synthetic/de-identified data first, professional review gates, specialist advice and bounded work packages.

The project plan appendix gives the Gantt chart, milestone deliverables and monthly payment schedule.

12. Commercial impacts
What is the commercial potential of your project?

The proposed customers are education settings, multi-academy trusts, local authorities, inclusion services, charities, parent support organisations and education technology providers that need earlier, fairer and more consistent evidence around SEN identification and strengths and needs assessment.

The route to market is staged. Phase 1 produces a safeguarded prototype, user evidence, validation report and adoption playbook. After phase 1, R&A will pursue paid pilots or phase 2 implementation with schools, local authorities, trusts, charities or public-sector innovation partners. Commercial delivery can be a supported software service, implementation package, evidence workflow licence, or integration layer for existing education and case-management systems.

The competitive advantage is auditability. Many alternatives focus on assessment content, records or AI output. Aureon focuses on evidence provenance, missing evidence, confidence, sensitivity, human review status and defensible handoff. This supports professional judgement instead of replacing it, which is important in a sensitive market involving children and families.

R&A has no existing commercial relationship that guarantees adoption for this project. That is why phase 1 includes co-design, user engagement and route-to-adoption work. The wider market includes public and private education, health-adjacent support pathways and international systems facing similar early identification challenges, but UK children and families are the direct or indirect beneficiaries of this phase.
