Project summary
Project summary

Aureon Evidence Studio is a local-first AI evidence automation platform for Createch teams working in advertising, marketing and adjacent creative production. Creative projects now rely on AI-assisted ideation, prompt chains, generated assets, code, partner files, approvals, claims and compliance records. The evidence behind those workflows is usually scattered across local folders, cloud drives, repositories, screenshots, emails and documents, making it hard to prove what was produced, what sources were used, what changed and what can be reused.

This industrial research project will develop a prototype evidence studio that ingests mixed project material, classifies source evidence, flags sensitive items, maps claims to supporting files and creates human-reviewed evidence packs for clients, funders, partners and quality assurance. The innovation is a controlled evidence-routing layer for AI-enabled creative work, with measurable scoring for source grounding, evidence completeness, version drift and safe handoff gates.

The project will use the existing Aureon software and research corpus as the testbed, then validate the prototype against realistic creative technology scenarios including AI-assisted campaign development, interactive asset and version tracking, technical documentation, and client or funder evidence packs. Outputs will include a demonstrator, evidence taxonomy, privacy rules, validation suite and commercial roadmap for SMEs in AI-enabled creative and technical production.

Public description
Public description

R&A Consulting and Brokerage Services Ltd will develop Aureon Evidence Studio, a digital platform for creative technology teams that need clearer evidence, audit trails and reusable project records when using AI-assisted production workflows.

The platform will help teams gather and structure the materials behind creative and technical work, including prompts, generated assets, design decisions, source files, code, approvals, screenshots and partner evidence. It will classify evidence, identify sensitive material, connect claims to source records and produce human-reviewed packs for client assurance, funding applications, internal quality control and partner handover.

The project focuses on advertising and marketing workflows where AI-generated concepts, campaign assets, copy, visuals, experiments and client claims must be reviewed quickly and responsibly. It will also be designed with cross-sector potential for film, TV, games, music, performing arts and visual arts teams that need to document complex digital production.

The planned outputs are a working prototype, source taxonomy, privacy and exclusion rules, validation tests, example workflows and a commercial plan for a reusable Createch productivity and assurance service. The project is intended to help SMEs reduce manual administration, improve evidence discipline, speed up client-ready reporting and build trust in AI-enabled creative workflows.

8. Need or challenge
What is the business need, technological challenge, or market opportunity behind your innovation?

Advertising and marketing teams are moving quickly into AI-assisted production, but the evidence behind that work is fragmented. A campaign may include briefs, prompts, generated assets, reference material, code, screenshots, client approvals, compliance notes, performance assumptions and partner files. These records usually sit across local folders, cloud drives, repositories, email and design tools. When the team needs to prove what was produced, why it was used, what sources informed it and what can safely be reused, the process becomes manual, slow and inconsistent.

The business need is stronger evidence discipline for AI-enabled creative workflows. Agencies and small creative technology teams must move fast, but they also need source traceability, human review, IP awareness, client assurance and reusable records for reporting or funding. Existing project management, DAM and collaboration tools organise tasks or files, but they do not provide a controlled evidence-routing layer that maps claims to sources, scores completeness and flags privacy or reuse risks.

R&A Consulting has already built a local Aureon software and research corpus around evidence automation, source ledgers, human-in-the-loop review and structured reporting. The proposed project converts that existing capability into a focused Createch product for advertising and marketing workflows. The opportunity is to give SMEs a practical way to make AI production more transparent, defensible and commercially repeatable, without turning the system into unsupported autonomous decision-making.

9. Approach and innovation
What approach will you take and where will the focus of the innovation be?

The project will develop Aureon Evidence Studio as a prototype digital platform for AI-enabled advertising and marketing workflows. The innovation is a controlled evidence layer that sits above scattered creative production material and turns it into reviewer-ready records. It will ingest mixed source material, classify evidence types, identify sensitive items, map claims to supporting files, score evidence completeness, detect version drift and create human-reviewed packs for clients, funders and internal quality assurance.

The approach will combine existing technologies in a new Createch application area with new know-how around evidence routing and review gates. The project will not create campaign content as its main output. It will create the tooling that allows creative teams to evidence, validate and reuse the work behind campaigns and AI-assisted creative experiments.

The work will improve on similar tools by combining source mapping, evidence scoring, privacy exclusions and human handoff in one workflow. Most current tools focus on asset storage, task management or AI generation. Aureon Evidence Studio will focus on proof: what source supports the claim, what changed, what is sensitive, what requires review and what can be packaged for a client or funder.

Expected outputs are a demonstrator, evidence taxonomy, source-ingestion prototype, scoring rules, privacy exclusion rules, validation tests, example advertising and marketing workflows, project documentation and a commercial launch plan. This fits R&A Consulting's current service line in AI operations, evidence automation and technical documentation, while creating a new product revenue stream.

11. Market awareness
What does the market or markets you are targeting look like?

The primary target market is UK advertising and marketing SMEs and creative technology teams that use AI-assisted workflows to create, test or document campaign concepts, creative assets, copy, visuals, technical prototypes and client-facing reports. Adjacent markets include film and TV development teams, games teams, music and performing arts organisations and visual arts producers where AI-enabled production creates similar evidence, source and approval challenges.

The UK Creative Industries Sector Plan identifies the creative industries as a major growth sector, contributing around GBP124 billion in GVA and supporting around 2.4 million jobs. Advertising and marketing is one of the major frontier industries named for this competition, and Createch is expected to become a high-growth part of the creative economy as creative businesses adopt advanced digital tools and services.

The market is fragmented. Large agencies may build internal assurance workflows, but SMEs often rely on generic file storage, spreadsheets, project management tools, design suites and manual documentation. Barriers to entry include trust, data handling, integration with existing workflows, perceived administrative burden and the need to prove that evidence automation saves time rather than adding process.

Aureon Evidence Studio will target early adopters who already feel the pressure of AI governance, client assurance, IP concerns and repeatable campaign reporting. The project will validate willingness to use and pay through prototype demonstrations, feedback interviews and evidence pack examples before wider launch.

13. Wider impacts
What impact might this project have outside the project team?

The project can benefit customers by reducing time spent reconstructing evidence after creative work is complete. A practical target for validation is a 30 to 50 percent reduction in manual evidence pack preparation time for test workflows, alongside improved completeness and fewer unsupported claims. This can help small agencies and creative teams deliver client-ready reports faster and compete with larger firms that can afford bespoke compliance or operations teams.

For the supply chain, the platform can improve handover between strategists, designers, developers, AI operators, clients and funders. It creates clearer records of sources, decisions, changes and review status. This can reduce duplicated work, strengthen trust and improve reuse of approved creative and technical assets.

The project supports government priorities around creative industries growth, digital adoption, responsible innovation and regional innovation. Regionally, the project is led from Northern Ireland and can contribute to a stronger local Createch capability by turning an existing Belfast-based software and research corpus into a market-facing product.

Environmental impacts are expected to be modest but positive where better evidence reuse reduces duplicated production work, repeated searches and unnecessary rework. The main negative impact is compute and storage usage, which will be managed by local-first processing, selective ingestion and retention rules. Social impacts include improved transparency around AI-assisted production, better inclusion for small teams that lack administrative support, and stronger evidence for responsible creative practice.

How will you manage your project effectively?

The project will be managed by R&A Consulting as lead applicant using six work packages over 12 months. WP1 Discovery and evidence model, GBP35,000, will define user workflows, evidence types, success criteria and safe data rules. Dependencies are sequential but overlapping: WP1 informs all work, WP2 enables WP3 and WP4, WP5 tests outputs from WP2 to WP4, and WP6 packages the validated results.

15. Risks
What are the main risks for this project?

The main technical risk is that mixed creative evidence is too varied to classify reliably. Mitigation: start with a defined advertising and marketing evidence taxonomy, use controlled test sets, measure classification quality and keep human review gates. A second technical risk is that evidence scoring could appear to make unsupported judgements. Mitigation: scores will be framed as review support, not automated truth, and every score will link to visible source evidence and reviewer notes.

The main data risk is accidental inclusion of private, client or sensitive material. Mitigation: use sanitised test data, exclusion rules, local-first processing, manual approval before export and a no-private-attachments rule for funder submissions. Commercial risk is weak willingness to pay from SMEs. Mitigation: test pilot workflows with target users early, validate time saved and create a service-supported route before full software scaling.

Managerial risk is delivery overload for a small lead team. Mitigation: use six bounded work packages, reserve budget for targeted subcontractors and track progress weekly. Financial risk is underestimating development or validation cost. Mitigation: keep scope focused on a demonstrator and evidence pack workflow, not a broad creative suite. Regulatory and ethical risk relates to AI, IP and client trust. Mitigation: privacy and IP review, transparent human review gates and clear user documentation.
