Abstract
The Position of Echo-F eedback Cognitive ￿-Substrate (PEFC￿S) framework [ 1] predicts that
coherent cognitive processing preserves phase-locked branches at frequencies spaced by pow-
ers of the golden ratio 𝜑 = (1 +√
5)/2 . Under this framework, inter-trial phase coherence
(ITPC) spectra of task-engaged neural recordings should exhibit peak structure enriched
near 𝑓𝑘= 𝑓0⋅ 𝜑𝑘beyond what uniform-spacing, integer-harmonic, or shuffled-peak null
models predict. This pre-registration locks the dataset panel, preprocessing pipeline, ITPC
computation, peak-detection algorithm, three null models, statistical inference procedure,
multiple-comparison correction, and confirmation thresholds before any confirmatory analy-
sis is conducted , following the Open Science F ramework pre-registration standard. The pro-

2 Hypotheses
2.1 Primary hypothesis (H1)
In task-engaged neural recordings meeting inclusion criteria, the ITPC spectrum exhibits peak
structure whose pairwise frequency ratios cluster closer to integer powers of 𝜑than predicted
by any of three null models (§ 5.4 ).
Null (H0): Peak structure shows no preferential spacing at powers of 𝜑over null distributions.
2.2 Secondary hypotheses
H2. ￿-enrichment is stronger in task-engaged epochs than within-subject resting baselines (paired
comparison where datasets provide both).
H3. ￿-enrichment is robust across task types — not specific to a single paradigm.
H4. ￿-enrichment scales with task-engagement indicators (reaction time residuals, accuracy)
where behavioural measures exist.

5 Analysis Plan
5.1 Preprocessing pipeline (fixed)
All preprocessing via MNE-Python v1.7+ with identical parameters across datasets:
1. Load BIDS-compliant recording via mne_bids.read_raw_bids
2. Notch filter at mains (50/60 Hz per metadata) and harmonics to 200 Hz
3. Band-pass 1.0–100 Hz, zero-phase FIR, Hamming window, default MNE parameters
4. Automated bad-channel detection via NoisyChannels (PREP port), interpolation
5. Common average reference (EEG) or signal-space separation (MEG)
6. ICA artefact rejection: F astICA, 30 components, auto EOG/ECG removal via ICALabel
7. Epoch around stimulus: 𝑡min = −0.5 s, 𝑡max = +2.0 s
8. Peak-to-peak rejection threshold: 150 ￿V (EEG) or 3 pT (MEG)
9. Minimum retained per subject/condition: 32 epochs, else exclude subject

5.3 Peak detection (fixed parameters)
Peak detection on 𝑧-scored ITPC spectrum via scipy.signal.find_peaks with:
• Prominence: 0.5 (𝑧-score units)
• Minimum inter-peak distance: 3 frequency bins
• Minimum peak height: 0.5 (𝑧-score units)
These parameters are fixed by this pre-registration and will not be tuned on confirmatory data.

5.5 Statistical inference
Subject-level: Each subject contributes one triple (𝑅𝐴, 𝑅𝐵, 𝑅𝐶). F or each null, one-sample
Wilcoxon signed-rank test of log 𝑅𝜑vs 0 (log-transformed to normalise ratio distribution).
Dataset-level: Per-null Wilcoxon yields one 𝑝-value per dataset per null. Binomial test on
proportion of subjects with 𝑅𝜑> 1 provides a second dataset-level summary .

datasets, separately for each null model. Bonferroni correction across three nulls: 𝛼corrected =
0.05/3 = 0.0167 .

8 Exclusion Rules (Fixed)
A subject is excluded if any of:
• F ewer than 32 clean epochs after artefact rejection
• F ewer than 16 good channels after bad-channel detection
• Recording duration < 60 s continuous usable data
• F ewer than 4 ITPC peaks detected
• Missing stimulus-event markers preventing epoching
A dataset is excluded before confirmatory run if< 8 subjects remain after preprocessing. Ex-
cluded datasets are reported but do not contribute to the primary aggregate test.
9 Stopping Rules
All seven confirmatory datasets will be analysed in full, exactly once , following pre-registration.
No interim peeking. No data added after confirmatory run. No subjects added post-hoc. If a
dataset becomes unavailable between pre-registration and run, it is dropped and Bonferroni
recomputed; no substitution.
10 Deviations Policy
Any deviation from this pre-registration will be reported in a dedicated “Deviations” section of

and without the deviation.
11 Limitations Acknowledged Up F ront
1. ITPC peak detection is not fully scale-invariant across datasets with varying SNR; the same
parameters may yield different peak counts. Null A (shuffled peaks) controls for this.
2. 𝜑 is numerically close to some integer ratios ( 𝜑2≈ 2.618 near 3). Null B is specifically
designed to address this.
3. ITPC is not identical to phase-locked value ΓPL V as defined in PEFC￿S. ITPC is the closest
standard EEG observable; the theoretical mapping is documented in [ 1].

from__future__ import annotations
import math
import numpy as np
import mne
frommne_bids import BIDSPath, read_raw_bids
frommne.time_frequency import tfr_morlet
fromscipy.signal import find_peaks
fromscipy.stats import wilcoxon, combine_pvalues
PHI = 1.6180339887498949
LOG_PHI = math.log(PHI)
# --- preprocess - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - -
defpreprocess(raw, line_freq):
raw.notch_filter(np.arange(line_freq, 200 + line_freq, line_freq),
fir_design= "firwin")
raw. filter(1.0, 100.0, fir_design= "firwin")
raw.set_eeg_reference( "average ", projection=True)
ica = mne.preprocessing.ICA(n_components=30, random_state=42,
method= "fastica ", max_iter= "auto")

ica.fit(raw)
eog_idx, _ = ica.find_bads_eog(raw)
ecg_idx, _ = ica.find_bads_ecg(raw)
ica.exclude = list(set(eog_idx + ecg_idx))
return ica. apply(raw)
# --- ITPC - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - -
defcompute_itpc(epochs):
freqs = np.logspace(np.log10(2), np.log10(80), 60)
n_cycles = freqs / 2.0
_, itc = tfr_morlet(epochs, freqs=freqs, n_cycles=n_cycles,
return_itc=True, average=True, decim=4,
n_jobs=-1, verbose= "error")
return freqs, itc.data.mean(axis=(0, 2))
# --- peak detection - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - -
defdetect_peaks(itpc_spec, freqs):
z = (itpc_spec - itpc_spec.mean()) / itpc_spec.std()
peaks, _ = find_peaks(z, prominence=0.5, distance=3, height=0.5)
return freqs[peaks]
# --- phi -enrichment - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - -
defphi_enrichment(peak_freqs, n_boot=10000, rng=None):
rng = rng ornp.random.default_rng(42)
peak_freqs = np.asarray(peak_freqs)
if len(peak_freqs) < 4:
return None
ratios = []
fori in range(len(peak_freqs)):
forj in range(i + 1, len(peak_freqs)):
ifpeak_freqs[i] > 0:
ratios.append(peak_freqs[j] / peak_freqs[i])
ratios = np.asarray(ratios)
log_r = np.log(ratios)
D_obs = np.mean(np. abs(log_r - np. round(log_r / LOG_PHI) * LOG_PHI))
# Null A: shuffled peaks
D_A = []
for_ in range(n_boot):
perm = rng.permutation(peak_freqs)
rs = [perm[j]/perm[i] fori in range(len(perm))
forj in range(i+1, len(perm)) ifperm[i] > 0]
lr = np.log(rs)
D_A.append(np.mean(np. abs(lr - np. round(lr/LOG_PHI) * LOG_PHI)))

# Null B: integer harmonics
uni = np.array([2,3,4,5,6,7,8,9], dtype= float)

"R_phi_A ": D_A / max(D_obs, 1e-12),
