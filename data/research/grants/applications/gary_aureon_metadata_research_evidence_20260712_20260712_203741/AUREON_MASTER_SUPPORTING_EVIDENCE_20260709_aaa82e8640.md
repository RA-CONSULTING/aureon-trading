## Programme Summary

The Aureon programme develops local-first evidence automation for AI-assisted research, SME operations, and grant-ready technical reporting. The work combines:

- source indexing and claim-to-evidence mapping;
- reproducible workflow records;
- software and research corpus organisation;
- human review and approval controls;
- privacy-aware exclusion of sensitive material;
- formal evidence packs for funders, advisers, partners, and customers.

The programme is being positioned across three fundable lines:

1. **Aureon Evidence OS for AI-driven scientific discovery**  
   Research workflow tooling for source classification, reproducible experiments, evidence routing, coherence scoring, and open technical reporting.

2. **Aureon Evidence Studio for Createch AI workflows**  
   A commercial prototype for advertising, marketing, and creative technology teams that need clear proof of source use, review status, approvals, and reuse boundaries.

3. **Aureon evidence automation for SME operations**  
   Practical evidence workflows for compliance, warehouse and stock records, back-office automation, grant reporting, and repeatable business documentation.

## Capability Evidence

The Aureon software and research corpus demonstrates a sustained body of work in evidence automation, AI-assisted workflow control, source-led reporting, and local-first technical operations. The strongest grant-ready capabilities are:

- structured evidence ingestion and classification;
- source-grounded report generation;

- repeatable project pack generation;
- validation planning and risk register creation.

## Research Positioning

Aureon research outputs are used as authored research material and testable hypotheses. They are not presented as independently validated scientific outcomes unless independent validation exists.

For scientific-discovery routes, HNC and related work are positioned as algorithmic design hypotheses for coherence scoring, signal analysis, evidence routing, and reproducibility experiments. This allows the programme to pursue ambitious research while keeping claims bounded, testable, and funder-safe.

## Governance And Data Handling

External grant packs are prepared from public, owned, licensed, or sanitised material. Private financial records, credentials, private emails, personal files, customer records, and unreviewed sensitive data are excluded from funder attachments by default.

Each external evidence pack should include:

- applicant identity;
- project scope;
- evidence source list;
- validation plan;
- delivery plan;
- responsible data handling statement;
- budget and partner assumptions where applicable.

## Attachment Guidance

For funder-facing submissions, attach the polished evidence summary relevant to the competition and any requested appendices. Use application-specific appendices where they are requested by the funder or adviser.
