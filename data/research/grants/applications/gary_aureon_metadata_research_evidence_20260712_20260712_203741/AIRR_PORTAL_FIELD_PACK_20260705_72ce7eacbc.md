## 1000-Character Project Summary

R&A Consulting and the Aureon Institute request 500,000 GPU hours over 12 months to develop and test Aureon Evidence OS: a local-first, auditable AI workflow layer for scientific discovery. The project will convert an existing open-source operator repository and public/local research corpus into reproducible experiments for source classification, evidence routing, coherence scoring, multi-agent workflow simulation, and report generation. HNC will be treated as a testable algorithmic hypothesis, not as a validated scientific claim. The outputs will be open-source workflow code, benchmark results, negative findings, a public technical report, and a reproducible demo showing how AI systems can gather, classify, validate, and package research evidence before any external action.

## Scored Assessment Responses

### 1. Alignment to Priority Areas and Credible Research Outcomes

The proposal aligns with the AI-driven research and scientific discovery priority area. It develops software tools and AI-assisted workflows that improve how AI systems gather, classify, validate, and reproduce scientific evidence. The intended outcome is not a black-box assistant but an auditable evidence operating layer: source inventory, claim classification, risk tagging, validation gates, and proof artifacts before external action.

Credible outputs are:

- Open-source workflow code and reproducible run instructions.
- Benchmark results comparing baseline evidence routing with HNC-inspired coherence scoring.
- A source-indexed corpus using claim classes A to D.
- A technical report including limitations and negative results.
- A lightweight demonstration for grant, research, and scientific discovery workflows.

### 2. Ambition, Novelty, and Why AIRR Is Needed

The novel contribution is a coherence-scored evidence operating system for AI workflows. Instead of asking a model to produce a final answer directly, Aureon routes work through source inventory, claim classing, validation checks, and human-review states. HNC is used as an algorithmic design hypothesis: whether golden-ratio-inspired temporal or spectral windows improve coherence scoring, task routing, or benchmark stability compared with conventional fixed windows.

AIRR is needed because local hardware is sufficient for prototypes but not for robust benchmark sweeps, ablation studies, multi-agent simulations, or repeated runs across models, seeds, datasets, and routing strategies. The compute request enables a credible comparison between conventional baselines and HNC-inspired workflow controls at a scale not possible locally.

### 3. Previous GPU / Compute Use and Scaling Dependency

The Aureon repos show substantial local-first software work, simulations, benchmark-style artifacts, agent orchestration, workflow control, and public/open-source development. The current limitation is scale: local machine experiments cannot provide enough repeated runs, dataset throughput, model-size variation, or seed coverage to make a credible claim about evidence-routing methods. AIRR compute is therefore not a convenience request; it is the difference between anecdotal local demonstrations and a reproducible, statistically meaningful evaluation.

The 500,000 GPU-hour request is allocated across:

- 50,000 hours: corpus ingestion and evidence graph experiments.
- 150,000 hours: coherence scoring benchmark sweeps.
- 150,000 hours: multi-agent workflow simulation.
- 100,000 hours: public dataset feasibility experiments.
- 50,000 hours: distillation, reproducibility checks, and public demo.

### 4. Ethical and Responsible Research and Innovation

The project will not run live trading, make financial decisions, process private health data, or execute external grant/email submissions during compute experiments. Datasets will be public, synthetic, or locally owned with licence checks. Human review will remain required for legal filings, emails, grant submissions, and any external action.

Risk controls:

- Data licence and provenance checks before ingestion.
- No private Gmail, Drive, secrets, customer data, or personal datasets in experiments.
- Claim classes to prevent speculative language being presented as validated evidence.
- Baseline comparisons and negative results included.
- Open methods and reproducibility instructions.
- Explicit separation of research hypothesis, prototype evidence, and validated claims.
