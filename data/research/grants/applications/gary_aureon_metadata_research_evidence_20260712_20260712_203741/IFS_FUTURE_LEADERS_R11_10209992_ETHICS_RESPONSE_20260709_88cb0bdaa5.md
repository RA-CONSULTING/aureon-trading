# Future Leaders Fellowships Round 11 - Additional Ethical Questions Response

Prepared by: Aureon  
Applicant: R&A Consulting and Brokerage Services Ltd  
Application number: 10209992  
Project title: Aureon Evidence OS for AI-Driven Scientific Discovery and Auditable SME Automation  
Date: 9 July 2026

## Project Summary

The project will develop a local-first evidence operating system for AI-driven scientific discovery workflows and auditable SME automation. The work will use public, owned, licensed, or sanitised material and will focus on source tracking, claim-to-evidence mapping, reproducibility, responsible AI governance, validation reporting, and SME evidence workflows.

## Animal Research And Animal Testing

The project does not involve animal research, animal testing, animal tissue, animal-derived experimental material, or animal trials.

## Human Participants

No clinical research, medical intervention, patient recruitment, or health-related human-participant study is planned. If later usability testing or SME workflow review is carried out, it will use proportionate consent, minimal data collection, privacy notices, and review appropriate to the activity before any participant engagement begins.

## Data Protection And Privacy

The project will use public, owned, licensed, or sanitised data by default. Credentials, private financial records, private emails, customer records, private communications, and sensitive personal data will be excluded from external outputs unless a clear lawful basis, permission, purpose, and review process are in place.

## Responsible AI And Research Integrity

Aureon will be designed to support human review rather than replace expert judgement. The system will preserve source provenance, classify claims, identify validation status, and distinguish evidence, assumptions, hypotheses, and unsupported claims. Authored research materials will be treated as hypotheses, simulation routes, or validation targets unless independently validated.

## Security, Misuse, And Trusted Research

The project is UK-hosted and local-first. Collaboration, publication, and data sharing will be reviewed for IP, confidentiality, data protection, security, export-control, and trusted-research considerations before release or external sharing.

## Overall Ethical Position

The ethical risk level is expected to be low to moderate for the planned work because the project is software and evidence-infrastructure focused, uses bounded claims, excludes animal testing, does not plan clinical or medical participant activity, and embeds privacy, provenance, human review, and responsible AI controls from the start.
