Project summary
Project summary

This fellowship will build Aureon Evidence OS: a local-first evidence operating system for AI-driven scientific discovery and auditable SME automation. The programme will convert Gary Leckey's existing software, research corpus and workflow records into reproducible tools for source classification, claim-to-evidence mapping, validation planning, and reviewer-ready reporting.

The work will combine industrial research in AI workflow assurance with practical SME demonstrators. It will produce a controlled runtime, evidence ledger, benchmark tasks, governance controls, and validation reports that show how AI-assisted research and business workflows can be made transparent, repeatable and safe to review.

R&A Consulting and Brokerage Services Ltd will host the fellowship in Northern Ireland through the Aureon Institute programme. The project will support UK capability in responsible AI, reproducible research infrastructure, SME digital adoption and high-integrity evidence handling.

Public description
Public description

Aureon Evidence OS will help researchers and SMEs turn complex digital work into clear, source-grounded evidence. The project will develop local-first software that records sources, maps claims to evidence, organises validation tasks and produces transparent reports for review.

The fellowship will focus on responsible AI workflows for scientific discovery and practical SME automation, using R&A Consulting and Brokerage Services Ltd's existing Aureon software and research corpus as the starting point. Outputs will include prototype software, validation reports, governance controls and public dissemination material.

12. Vision
What are your plans for the fellowship?

The fellowship vision is to establish Aureon Evidence OS as a UK-led evidence operating system for AI-driven scientific discovery and auditable SME automation. The core idea is that high-value research and business work increasingly depends on AI-assisted gathering, interpreting and reporting of evidence, but the evidence trail behind those outputs is often fragmented, hard to review and hard to reproduce. Aureon will address this by building a local-first software layer that records sources, maps claims to evidence, tracks validation status and produces reviewer-ready reports.

The programme will turn R&A Consulting and Brokerage Services Ltd's existing Aureon software, research corpus and workflow records into an externally validated research and innovation platform. It will focus on three linked outcomes. First, a technical runtime for source ingestion, claim classification, evidence routing, coherence scoring, audit trails and human review. Second, reproducible scientific-discovery workflows that convert authored research outputs and hypotheses into testable benchmark tasks, negative-result records and transparent reports. Third, SME automation demonstrators showing how the same evidence methods can support compliance, operational records, grant reporting, warehouse evidence and back-office review.

The fellowship will accelerate Gary Leckey's development from independent technical founder and research author into a recognised leader in responsible AI workflow assurance. The leadership challenge is not only to build software, but to create a discipline for evidence-led AI operations: clear boundaries between hypotheses and validated claims, transparent source use, privacy-aware data handling, and practical tooling that smaller organisations can adopt.

The UK benefit is a stronger capability for trustworthy AI adoption, research reproducibility and SME productivity. Northern Ireland is an appropriate base because the project combines digital innovation, small business needs, responsible AI practice and international research visibility from a UK region that can benefit from high-value technical leadership. By the end of the fellowship, Aureon should have a validated software prototype, a public technical evidence framework, partner-reviewed demonstrators, an IP and commercialisation route, and a leadership platform for continued UK exploitation.

13. Approach and innovation
What approach will you take and where will the focus of the innovation be?

The approach is to build Aureon Evidence OS through staged industrial research, validation and demonstrator work. The project will start from existing Aureon repositories, workflow scripts, research records and evidence packs, then turn them into a coherent software and research programme with clear review controls.

WP1 will define the Evidence OS core. This includes source indexing, claim-to-evidence mapping, evidence status labels, local storage rules, privacy exclusions, review records and report generation. The innovation focus is the combination of local-first AI workflow control with grant, research and operational evidence ledgers. The goal is not to replace expert judgement, but to make AI-assisted work inspectable and repeatable.

WP2 will create scientific-discovery workflows. HNC, PEFC, EPAS and related authored research material will be treated as hypotheses, simulation routes or validation targets. The project will convert them into reproducible tasks: source classification, coherence scoring, benchmark comparison, falsification protocols, negative findings and technical reporting. This creates a credible pathway from authored material to testable outputs.

WP3 will build SME automation demonstrators. These will focus on evidence-heavy workflows such as compliance records, warehouse or stock proof, grant reporting and back-office documentation. The demonstrators will measure time saved, completeness of evidence packs, review status clarity and reduction in unsupported claims.

WP4 will run external validation and challenge. The project will seek review from technical, academic, SME and governance advisers. Validation will include benchmark tasks, usability review, claim-quality scoring and stress-testing of the system's ability to separate evidence, assumptions and unsupported claims.

WP5 will prepare exploitation. This will cover product architecture, IP review, data protection, open technical reporting, partner pipeline, pricing and a responsible commercial route.

The innovation sits in the structured evidence layer: a practical bridge between AI tools, research workflows, software repositories and funder/customer-ready evidence. Current tools can generate text or manage documents, but Aureon is designed to preserve why a claim is made, where the source came from, what has been checked and what remains unverified.

17. Ethics and responsible research and innovation (RRI)
What are the ethical and RRI implications and issues relating to the proposed work?

The main ethical and responsible research issues are evidence integrity, AI transparency, privacy, data protection, IP, bias and possible misuse of automated reporting. Aureon will address these by designing the system around source tracking, claim classification, human review and clear separation between evidence, assumptions, hypotheses and unsupported claims.

The project will use public, owned, licensed or sanitised data. Credentials, private financial records, customer records, private emails and sensitive personal data will be excluded from external outputs unless a specific lawful basis, review and approval process is in place.

The system will not be presented as replacing expert judgement. It will support review by making evidence trails clearer. Research materials such as HNC, PEFC and EPAS will be treated as authored research outputs, hypotheses, simulation routes or validation targets unless independently validated.

The project does not plan animal testing. Human participation, if later required for usability or SME workflow testing, will be handled through consent, minimal-data collection, privacy notices and ethical review proportionate to the activity.

18. Project management
How will you manage your project effectively?

The project will be managed as a four-year programme with staged work packages, evidence milestones and review points. R&A Consulting will maintain a project board covering technical delivery, evidence quality, finance, risk, data handling, IP and dissemination.

Year 1 will focus on Evidence OS architecture, source and claim taxonomy, governance rules and baseline prototypes. Year 2 will build scientific-discovery workflows, benchmark tasks and validation methods. Year 3 will develop SME automation demonstrators and external review. Year 4 will consolidate validation, public reporting, product architecture, exploitation planning and follow-on funding or commercial routes.

Each work package will have defined outputs, acceptance criteria and evidence records. Progress will be tracked through repository history, issue logs, evidence registers, prototype demonstrations, validation reports and partner/adviser feedback.

Decision points will test whether the project is ready to move from design to prototype, from prototype to validation, and from validation to exploitation. Risks will be reviewed regularly, with corrective action recorded. Finance and procurement will follow eligible cost and value-for-money rules.

19. Risks
What are the main risks for this project?

The main technical risk is that the Evidence OS becomes too broad. This will be managed by staged scope control: core evidence ledger first, then scientific workflows and SME demonstrators.

The main evidence risk is overstating early-stage research. This will be managed through claim classification, validation status labels, negative-result records and external challenge. Authored research will be presented as hypotheses or validation targets unless independent evidence supports stronger claims.

The main delivery risk is limited specialist capacity. This will be managed by using advisers, subcontractors or partners for targeted software review, data governance, IP, finance and validation support.

The main data risk is inappropriate use of sensitive material. Aureon will use public, owned or sanitised data by default, with explicit exclusions for credentials, private finance, personal records and private communications.

The main commercial risk is that SMEs do not adopt a complex evidence system. This will be managed by testing practical workflows, measuring time saving and completeness, and designing simple outputs that fit grant reporting, compliance and operational review needs.

22. Data Management and Sharing
How will you manage and share data collected or acquired through the proposed research?

Aureon will manage data using a local-first, evidence-led model. Data sources will be classified before use as public, owned, licensed, partner-provided, sensitive or excluded. The default evidence base will be public or owned material, local software repositories, research outputs, generated validation records, project logs, benchmark results and sanitised SME workflow examples.

Sensitive material will be minimised. Credentials, private financial records, private emails, customer records, personal files and unreviewed confidential material will be excluded from external outputs unless there is a clear lawful basis, permission, purpose and review process.

The project will record source provenance, access permissions, claim links, review status, retention decisions and publication status. Data used for benchmarks and reports will be versioned so that results can be reproduced or challenged. Negative findings and limitations will be recorded alongside successful outputs.

Sharing will be proportionate. Public outputs may include technical reports, non-sensitive datasets, benchmark descriptions, validation methods and selected open-source code. Commercially sensitive code, private company material and personal data will not be openly released. Any partner data will be governed by written terms covering access, use, IP, confidentiality, security, retention and publication.

The aim is to make evidence more transparent without exposing private or sensitive material.
