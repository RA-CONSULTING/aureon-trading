## Project Summary

R&A Consulting and the Aureon Institute request compute access to develop and test Aureon Evidence OS, a local-first AI evidence operating layer for scientific-discovery workflows.

The project converts an existing Aureon software and research corpus into reproducible experiments for:

- source classification;
- evidence routing;
- coherence scoring;
- multi-agent workflow simulation;
- report generation;
- benchmarked comparison against conventional baselines.

HNC and related research outputs are used as testable algorithmic hypotheses. The project does not present them as independently validated scientific outcomes.

## Why AIRR Compute Is Needed

Local hardware is sufficient for prototypes and small demonstrations, but not for repeated benchmark sweeps, ablation studies, multi-agent simulations, or reproducibility tests across models, seeds, datasets, and routing strategies.

## Responsible Research And Data Handling

The project will use public, synthetic, owned, or licensed datasets. Private Gmail content, Drive files, credentials, financial records, customer records, private health data, and sensitive personal data are outside the compute experiment scope.

The intended outputs are:

- open-source workflow code;
- reproducible run instructions;
- benchmark results;
- negative findings where applicable;
- a public technical report;
- a lightweight demonstration for research evidence workflows.

## Expected Value

The project supports UKRI's AI-driven scientific discovery priority by improving the way AI systems gather, classify, validate, and reproduce evidence. The outcome is not a black-box assistant. It is an auditable workflow layer that helps research and innovation teams understand what evidence supports a claim, what remains uncertain, and what can be shared safely.
