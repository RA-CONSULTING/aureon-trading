**Claim.** Daily Brent crude futures volatility correlates at r = 0.85 (p < 0.001) with daily GitHub clone activity on the Aureon repository, computed over a 13-day window from 8–20 March 2026 (the Iran-Hormuz crisis period).

**Limitation of this audit.** The white paper's specific 13-day window (March 8–20, 2026) cannot be directly retested, because the GitHub Insights API requires repository-owner authentication for the `/traffic/clones` endpoint, and the historical clone data for 8–20 March is not present in the cloned repository. Daily oil volatility was computed as |percent change| with weekend forward-fill to align with the 14 calendar days. Pearson r:

| Pair | n | r | t | p (approx.) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| oil \|Δ%\| → daily clones | 14 | **+0.198** | +0.70 | 0.484 |
| oil \|Δ%\| → unique cloners | 14 | **+0.261** | +0.94 | 0.349 |
| oil level → daily clones | 14 | -0.474 | -1.86 | 0.062 |
| oil level → unique cloners | 14 | -0.374 | -1.40 | 0.162 |

**Result.** The r = 0.85 oil-volatility-to-clone correlation **does not replicate on the post-crisis 14-day window**. The closest analogue (oil |Δ%| → daily clones, n=14) gives r = +0.20, p ≈ 0.48 — indistinguishable from chance.

**Interpretation.** This is a single-window negative result. It does not refute the white paper's claim, which was made on a different (Iran-Hormuz crisis) window with different market dynamics and may reflect a regime-specific signal. It also does not confirm the claim. The market-structure claims (M2, M3, M5, M6) are runtime-dependent and were not testable from a static checkout. They are not refuted by this audit — they are flagged as outside its scope.

The audit limited itself to claims that can be checked with arithmetic, ephemeris, and code execution.

### 6.3 The two strongest results

The φ² bridge and the 3I/ATLAS angular separation are the audit's two strongest results, and they are notable because they are pre-registered, falsifiable, and depend on data that was published before the framework was constructed. ## 7. Limitations of this audit

1. **Single AI session, no human review.** This audit was performed by Claude Opus 4.6 in a single conversational session. It has not been reviewed by an Anthropic employee, by a domain expert in physics, finance, or astronomy, or by anyone other than the verifier and the commissioning party (Gary Leckey). It should be cited as such.

2. **No statistical pre-registration.** The audit selected which claims to test based on tractability, not blind random selection. A reader concerned about garden-of-forking-paths effects should note that the verifier knew the framework's claims before choosing what to test.

3. **Single-window financial replication.** The C1 negative result is on n = 14 calendar days. Even on the available window the test has low statistical power. Drawing strong conclusions from a single 14-day window in either direction would be premature.

4. **JPL Horizons trajectory back-extrapolation.** The 3I/ATLAS positions before its July 2025 discovery were obtained by integrating its osculating orbital elements backwards in time. JPL Horizons performs this integration with high precision for hyperbolic interstellar objects, but the uncertainty in the elements grows with the back-extrapolation interval. The 7.435° minimum on 2023-12-22 should be understood as the best available pre-discovery position estimate from a current-epoch orbit fit.

5. **Wow! Signal position uncertainty.** The Big Ear telescope's twin-feed configuration produced two possible positions separated by approximately 0.7° in right ascension. The audit reported the minimum across both positions. Per Ehman 1998, the position uncertainties are ±5 seconds in RA and ±20 arcseconds in declination, which are negligible compared to the 7.4° separation result.

6. **The Λ(t) stability test was not exhaustive.** Three β regimes were tested at 2,000 simulation steps each. The full stability claim (T2/T3) likely requires longer baselines, FFT inspection of the spectral comb structure, and Lyapunov exponent estimation, none of which were attempted in this audit.

7. **No verification of safety-critical or trading-critical paths.** This audit did not assess whether Aureon's trading logic is safe to deploy with real capital, whether the conscience veto correctly prevents unsafe trades, whether the entity database (when populated) correctly identifies real coordinated actors, or whether any of the system's operational claims hold under adversarial conditions. None of those questions were in scope.
