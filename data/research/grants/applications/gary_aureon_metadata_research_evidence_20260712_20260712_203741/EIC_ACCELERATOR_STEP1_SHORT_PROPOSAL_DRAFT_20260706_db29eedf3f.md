## 1. Problem and urgency

Creative, marketing and AI-enabled service teams are adopting generative and automation tools faster than their evidence practices can keep up. A single campaign or client deliverable can include briefs, prompts, generated outputs, screenshots, code, reference assets, approvals, performance assumptions and compliance notes spread across local folders, cloud drives, repositories and email.

When a customer, funder, auditor or partner asks what was produced, what sources supported it, what changed, what was reviewed and what can be safely reused, small teams often rebuild the proof trail manually. This is slow, inconsistent and weakens trust in AI-assisted production.

The timing is material because AI adoption is becoming normal in creative and digital service work, while customers are asking for stronger proof around IP, privacy, source reuse, quality assurance and human accountability. SMEs need evidence discipline without enterprise compliance overhead.

## 2. Innovation

Aureon Evidence Studio is proposed as a local-first evidence automation platform for AI-enabled creative and operational workflows. It does not generate campaign content as the main output. It turns scattered production material into source-grounded, reviewer-ready evidence packs.

The innovation is a controlled evidence layer combining source ingestion, evidence taxonomy, claim-to-source mapping, completeness scoring, sensitive-data exclusion, version drift checks and human review gates in one repeatable workflow.

Current tools tend to focus on project management, digital asset storage, AI generation, or generic document collaboration. Aureon focuses on proof: what supports this claim, which source is reusable, what changed, what is sensitive, what needs human review, and what package can be shared safely.

Indicative work packages: WP1 evidence taxonomy and safe data rules; WP2 ingestion and classification prototype; WP3 source-grounding and privacy controls; WP4 workflow demonstrator; WP5 pilot validation; WP6 commercial readiness and internationalisation plan.

## 4. Market and customers

Initial customers are SMEs and technical producers in advertising, marketing, creative technology, digital services and adjacent media workflows. These customers need faster client reporting, clearer AI-use records, safer source reuse and more defensible handover packs.

The beachhead is not broad AI governance for large enterprises. It is practical evidence operations for small teams that need to prove work without adding a full compliance department.

Commercial route: consultancy-supported pilots, paid evidence-pack generation, then recurring software access for teams that repeatedly need source-grounded delivery records. The internationalisation case should be built around EU and UK creative SMEs with similar AI proof needs.

## 5. Business model and funding need

Revenue model: setup and workflow mapping fees, monthly platform access, evidence-pack generation fees, and premium assurance support for larger or regulated clients.

Indicative EIC use of funds should focus on prototype hardening, validation, user pilots, privacy/IP review, commercial packaging, technical hires and EU market development. Do not request equity or blended finance until company structure, investor readiness and IP position are reviewed.

A grant-focused path should demonstrate why public support is needed: the hard work is not a normal agency tool build; it requires validation, safe handling rules, source traceability, human review design and commercial proof across real workflows before private investment is realistic.

## 7. Impact

Target customer impact: reduce manual evidence-pack preparation time by 30 to 50 percent in pilot workflows, improve evidence completeness, reduce unsupported claims, and make AI-assisted production easier to review and reuse safely.

Economic impact: create a product revenue stream for R&A Consulting, support adoption of responsible AI operations in creative SMEs, and build a Northern Ireland-led Createch capability with export potential.

Social and trust impact: improve transparency around AI-assisted production, strengthen human review, and help smaller teams compete with larger organisations that can afford bespoke operations and compliance functions.

## 8. Risks and mitigations

Technical risk: mixed creative evidence is hard to classify. Mitigation: start with a bounded taxonomy, controlled pilot data and human review gates.

Market risk: SMEs may not pay for assurance software. Mitigation: validate with service-supported pilots and price against time saved, client confidence and reduced rework.

Data risk: private or sensitive material could be included in exports. Mitigation: local-first processing, exclusion rules, no-private-attachment policy, manual approval before export and privacy/IP review.

EIC fit risk: EIC expects high-risk, high-potential innovation. Mitigation: do not submit until TRL evidence, pilot letters, competitor map, freedom-to-operate notes and growth plan are strong enough.
